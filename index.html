About the Department

The Utopia Police Department is the primary law enforcement agency serving the community of Utopia, Texas, located in Uvalde County. With a population of approximately 225 residents, Utopia is one of the smallest communities in the Texas Hill Country. Our department is committed to maintaining the safety, peace, and quality of life that makes Utopia a special place to live.

Our officers patrol the town and surrounding areas, respond to calls for service, and work closely with Uvalde County law enforcement to ensure comprehensive coverage across the region. We believe in community-oriented policing and strive to build strong relationships with the residents we serve.

Mission Statement

The mission of the Utopia Police Department is to protect and serve the citizens of Utopia, Texas through professional, ethical, and community-oriented law enforcement. We are dedicated to preserving the peace, preventing crime, and ensuring the safety of all residents and visitors.

Divisions & Services

Patrol Division

The Patrol Division is the backbone of the Utopia Police Department. Officers provide 24-hour patrol coverage throughout the town and respond to emergency and non-emergency calls for service. Duties include traffic enforcement, property checks, and maintaining a visible law enforcement presence in the community.

Community Outreach

Our Community Outreach program focuses on building trust and positive relationships between law enforcement and residents. Programs include school visits, community meetings, safety workshops, and the Neighborhood Watch initiative.

Crime Prevention

The department offers crime prevention services including residential security assessments, property check requests for traveling residents, and educational materials on personal and property safety. Residents can request a security survey by contacting our office.

Traffic Safety

Officers monitor roadways in and around Utopia to ensure the safety of motorists, cyclists, and pedestrians. Services include speed enforcement, accident investigation and reporting, and school zone monitoring.

Records & Reports

The Records Division maintains all police reports, case documentation, and public records. Requests for copies of police reports or public records can be submitted by email to records@police.utopiatx.org or in person at the police station during administrative hours.

Resources

File a Report

To file a non-emergency police report, visit the station during administrative hours or email records@police.utopiatx.org. For emergencies, always dial 911.

Neighborhood Watch

The Utopia Neighborhood Watch is a community partnership program coordinated by the police department. Interested residents can contact us to join or start a watch group in their area.

Careers

The Utopia Police Department accepts applications for sworn and non-sworn positions on an ongoing basis. Applicants must meet TCOLE (Texas Commission on Law Enforcement) standards. For current openings and application procedures, contact chief@police.utopiatx.org.

Safety Tips

  • Lock your vehicles and remove valuables from sight.
  • Report suspicious activity to our non-emergency line or tip email.
  • Keep exterior lights on at night for property security.
  • Know your neighbors and look out for one another.
  • Program non-emergency and emergency numbers into your phone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I request a copy of a police report?

You can request a copy of a police report by emailing records@police.utopiatx.org or visiting the station during administrative hours (Mon-Fri, 8 AM - 5 PM).

How do I submit an anonymous tip?

Anonymous tips can be submitted by email to tips@police.utopiatx.org. All tips are kept confidential.

Does the department coordinate with county law enforcement?

Yes, the Utopia Police Department works closely with the Uvalde County Sheriff's Office and other regional agencies to provide comprehensive law enforcement services throughout the area.

How can I join the Neighborhood Watch?

Contact the department at info@police.utopiatx.org or call our non-emergency number for information about joining or starting a Neighborhood Watch group in your area.
